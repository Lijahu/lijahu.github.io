Current dissertation

Multi-DoF Proprioceptive Feedback Device

Designing and building a multi-degree-of-freedom device for real-time proprioceptive feedback in upper-limb prosthesis applications, with emphasis on grasp identification.

What I worked on

  • System architecture and concept definition.
  • Hardware prototyping and iterative mechanical design.
  • Experimental validation strategy for embodied feedback.

What mattered

Full-stack engineering: device concept, sensing logic, physical build constraints, and human-centred testing.

Human-centred design

Sensory Substitution Insole for Peripheral Neuropathy

Designed a closed-loop feedback system using plantar pressure sensing, vibrotactile actuation, ultrasonic sensing, and drop-foot assistance concepts.

Design focus

  • Clinical need and user requirements.
  • Feedback pathway design and actuator selection.
  • Prototype development under safety and hardware constraints.

Why it stands out

Combined rehabilitation thinking with practical embedded-system design rather than a purely theoretical exercise.

Methods used
